Yishu Zhou (周祎舒)

I am a PhD candidate at the Department of Applied Physics at Yale University, under the guidance of Prof. Peter Rakich. My research is primarily centered around integrated photonics, microwave photonics, acoustics, and quantum nonlinear optics. Specifically, I delve into: i) Harnessing optical nonlinearities and light-matter interactions to pioneer the development of state-of-the-art integrated photonic and microwave photonic devices; ii) Designing innovative integrated photonic systems to advance our understanding of nonlinear optics and quantum optomechanics. Before joining Yale, I completed my undergraduate studies in Huazhong University of Science and Technology, where my research focused on wearable energy harvesting devices.
